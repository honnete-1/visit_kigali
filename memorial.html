Kigali Genocide Memorial

A respectful memorial site in Gisozi where visitors learn about the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi and Rwanda's journey of remembrance and unity.

Visit with respect

This page presents the memorial as an educational and reflective destination. Visitors should use quiet voices, follow site rules, avoid disruptive photography, and treat the memorial gardens with dignity.

  • Purpose: remembrance, education, peace-building, and reflection.
  • Recommended behavior: quiet, respectful, and patient.
  • Good for: students, visitors, researchers, and anyone learning Rwanda's history.
Tip: allocate enough time. This is not a place to rush through quickly.
