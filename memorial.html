Kigali Genocide Memorial

A sacred place of remembrance, learning, and hope — honoring the memory of those lost while celebrating Rwanda's remarkable renewal.

A Place of Memory and Healing

The Kigali Genocide Memorial (KGM) is situated in the Gisozi sector of Kigali, on a hill overlooking the city. It serves as the final resting place for more than 250,000 victims of the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.

The memorial is not only a cemetery but also an educational center that documents the genocide through exhibitions, archives, and research facilities. It is a place for reflection, learning, and healing — a testament to Rwanda's commitment to "Never Again."

Visitors from around world come to pay their respects, learn about this dark chapter in human history, and witness Rwanda's extraordinary journey from tragedy to renewal. The memorial gardens, with their carefully tended lawns and flowering trees, symbolize hope and regeneration.

What You Will Experience

The memorial complex includes several key areas that offer different perspectives on the genocide and Rwanda's recovery:

The Main Memorial: A mass grave containing the remains of over 250,000 victims, marked by a simple yet powerful inscription. The site is surrounded by gardens planted with agapanthus, whose purple flowers bloom each year around the anniversary.

Exhibition Hall: A comprehensive exhibition using photographs, personal belongings, and testimonies to tell the story of the genocide. It documents the events leading up to the genocide, the 100 days of violence, and Rwanda's remarkable recovery.

Children's Memorial: A dedicated space honoring the thousands of children who lost their lives, featuring photographs and messages from around the world.

Why Visit the Memorial

🕯️

Pay Respect

A sacred space to honor the memory of those who perished. Many visitors leave flowers and light candles at the memorial walls.

📚

Learn History

Comprehensive exhibitions document the genocide and its aftermath. The memorial serves as an important educational resource for students and researchers.

🌱

See Renewal

Witness how Rwanda has transformed tragedy into hope. The memorial is a powerful symbol of reconciliation and national unity.

Visitor Information

🕐 Opening Hours

  • Monday – Friday: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Saturday: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM
  • Sunday: Closed
  • April 7: Closed (Memorial Day)

📍 Location

  • Gisozi Sector, Kigali
  • About 4 km from city center
  • Taxi or moto available from downtown
  • Free parking on site

💰 Admission

  • Foreign visitors: $10
  • East African residents: RwF 2,000
  • Rwandan citizens: Free
  • Guided tours available

📝 Tips for Visitors

  • Allow 2-3 hours for your visit
  • Dress modestly and respectfully
  • Photography allowed in gardens only
  • No flash in exhibition halls
